# Journal: Additional Post Topics

To launch the Journal section with more than one post. Each includes a
working title, angle, and a short outline. Hyphen free throughout, written
to match the tone of the timber frame piece.

## 1. How Long Does a Timber Frame Home Actually Last?

**Angle:** Directly answers the "is wood durable enough" objection, which is
the single biggest hesitation people have about timber construction. Strong
SEO target since "how long do timber frame homes last" is a common search.

**Outline:**
- Open with the misconception: people assume wood means short lived
- Explain that properly engineered, treated timber frames regularly outlast
  100 years, with examples (centuries old timber framed buildings still
  standing across Europe)
- Cover what actually affects lifespan: moisture control, treatment,
  ventilation, build quality, not the material itself
- Compare maintenance needs against concrete and masonry, which also require
  upkeep (cracking, spalling, water ingress) and are often assumed
  maintenance free incorrectly
- Close on how proper design and craftsmanship (your craftsmanship) is the
  real determining factor in longevity, more than the material choice itself
- Soft CTA: durability built in from day one through proper engineering

## 2. Timber Frame vs Traditional Construction: What Is the Real Cost Difference?

**Angle:** Cost is the second biggest objection after durability. People
assume timber is either much cheaper or much more expensive, and the truth
is more nuanced. High search intent topic.

**Outline:**
- Address upfront cost vs lifetime cost as two different questions
- Break down where timber saves money: faster build times mean lower labor
  costs overall, less material waste, lower energy bills for decades after
  move in
- Break down where costs can be similar or higher: high end timber, complex
  architectural design, certain finishes
- Use a simple comparison framing, not exact pricing (pricing varies too
  much by project and region to commit to in writing), but actual ballpark
  ranges from Eco Vista projects could work well here if available
- Reframe the conversation from sticker price to total cost of ownership over
  20 to 30 years
- Soft CTA: book a consultation to get real numbers specific to a project

## 3. What Makes a Timber Frame Design Feel Modern, Not Rustic?

**Angle:** Directly supports your "modern architecture firm" brand
positioning and Gallery page. Less about technical/environmental benefits,
more about design philosophy, good for showcasing your portfolio and visual
work, strong for social sharing.

**Outline:**
- Open with the same misconception addressed briefly in the first article,
  but go deeper here: timber does not have to mean log cabin or cottage
  aesthetic
- Cover the actual design choices that create a modern feel: clean structural
  lines, large glazing, minimal ornamentation, monochrome or muted material
  palettes, exposed structural beams used intentionally rather than
  decoratively
- Reference how architecture firms globally are using timber in
  contemporary, award winning structures, not just residential cabins
- Tie back to Eco Vista's own design philosophy and portfolio, link directly
  to the Gallery page
- Heavy on imagery if published, light on stats, this is the most visual of
  the four planned posts
- Soft CTA: view the gallery or start a design conversation
